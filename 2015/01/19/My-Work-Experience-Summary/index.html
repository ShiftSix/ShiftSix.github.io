My Work Experience Summary

By Lian.Liu

Jan 19 2015 Updated:Jan 19 2015

Contents

This is an assignment from our Internship Assessment course. We need to write a brief summary of of internship or work experience, in which we are required to explain the reasons of taking this job, introduce the company as well as the position, and the takeaways.

In my fourth year of college, I decided not to keep pursuing academic study in microelectronics after graduation; instead, I realized that I was more interested in meeting different people and intrigued with their unique qualities. So I believed that a marketing position would both fulfill my interest and make use of my strength. Although it was not a small transformation from engineering to an interpersonal job, I still got an internship in a corporate communication department due to my experience in the campus TV station and other students’ activities. This position provided me with a basic understanding of the operation of an international company, strengthened my communication skills, and confirmed my determination to work in a marketing department. After the six-month internship, I applied for a marketing executive position with Henkel China. This position was mainly responsible for online marketing in the Greater China area. Thanks to my internship experience and the decently conducted interviews, I finally got this job.

Henkel is a German chemical company well known for its adhesive products and home care cosmetics. In China, Henkel has shown strong growth in the industrial adhesives market since 2000. The new century witnessed an increasing number of companies benefiting from the rapid development of the internet, which made Henkel, a traditional industrial business, also consider keeping up with the times. Therefore, Henkel China decided to establish an e-business function in its marketing department; and I was honored to be hired, mainly in charge of this function.

Initially my job included maintaining the corporate websites and conducting online campaigns. But just as the rapid growth of the internet, new problems related to online issues kept springing up unexpectedly. During the first two years of this job, I got the chance to implement various projects such as search engine optimization, online authentication and ecommerce management. Some of these projects were entirely new to the company. Luckily my boss always encouraged us to learn from different sources, which made this job challenging and worthwhile. As time went on, I became more familiar with the company operation and then was appointed to also take responsibility for the media relation issues. Contact with media was more complicated than I expected, since media agencies tended to hold their own values and ideas tightly. It required thorough communication tactics to consociate journalists from different agencies to benefit the company’s reputation.

One of the most important things I have learned from my three-year work experience is to always prepare yourself to adapt to this changing society. Even when you feel that everything is under control, it is better to get equipped for what might happen next. I can still remember that when I first entered the e-business field in 2011, basically most of the job was to promote the corporate websites and to maintain email marketing. But it only took less than a year for the company to shift its emphasis to mobile devices and ecommerce. During the second and third years, the boundary between online of offline marketing became more and more ambiguous; integrated marketing required collaboration from all functions of the marketing department. Consequently, my responsibilities changed as well.

Another takeaway I received from this job was how to balance various voices in the work place. Comparably, academic study is more straightforward since you only need to consider gaining good grades; and most of the time you just interact with classmates and professors. While during work, generally there is more than one project going on; and they usually involve tangling with different departments and bureaus who have their own interests and purposes. A coordinator in these kinds of projects should not only assure all voiced are heard, but also strive for greater benefits for the company.
