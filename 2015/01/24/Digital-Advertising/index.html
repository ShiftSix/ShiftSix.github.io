Digital Advertising

By Lian.Liu

Jan 24 2015 Updated:Jan 24 2015

Contents

With the rising concerns about safety and privacy issues of big data analysis on the internet, do you think digital advertising will be seriously affected by policies or regulations in the near future? If no, why; if yes, what do you think should this industry do to prepare for this situation?

This was the question I asked to Mr. Parikh in today’s seminar, the Marketing Scientist from MaxPoint. Due to my last job in digital marketing, I am always attracted by this topic. The real-time online analysis is completely changing the way of campaigns and will bring us undistracted advertising experience online. As long as it keeps anonymous, it is a most efficient way to target customers and win profits. But yes, more people are worrying about this rapid developing technology. Despite their ignorance of this technology itself, stories like iCloud leak and Edward Snowden just proved the growing risks of online information collection.
Asking me, I think it will but I do not know what to do. I was born in China I never fight against policies :) That is why I asked Mr. Parikh. With several years experience in digital advertising I believed he would have some deep insights on this question.
And he did.
“I wish it won’t”, he smiled. But it is possible; especially we have seen some similar cases in other countries already. For example Europe has the most strict Cookie Laws. And you can barely use Google Analytics in China due to regional restrictions. Why didn’t I think about this before?! I had been suffering from it! Even though the situation could be cruel, the bright side is policies having such heavy influence are usually implemented gradually, which leaves the industry adequate time to prepare. Then we will react accordingly. It is undeniable that to make money, and with the money on hand, companies need to make advertisements. Internet is the most convenient, accurate and efficient way to do it. So we will follow this path and strive for a solution.

I am pretty contented with this answer. It not only cleared my doubts of this industry, but also showed me a meticulous way to think. When I said yes, I believe digital advertising will be affected by policies; it was more like my gut: it caused crisis; it could be dangerous; so people are fighting against it. But he looked at a bigger picture; referred to historical and ongoing cases, which would lead the way to solutions. The logic is easy to follow and convincing, making me think I could come up with that as well. But if I could, why would I ask ;)
The environment I grew up in makes me worry about asking questions. What if it is a naive question? What if I actually can solve it myself? What if the other person refuses to answer? Now you see, asking to a mature thinker teaches you to become a mature thinker.

And by the way, this one-hour seminar was almost filled with students’ questions from all aspects, career, MEM Program, industry, etc. Mr. Parikh insightfully answered all questions without any preparation. Very impressive.
