Marketing was quite an inspiring class for me, even considering that I had been working with a competitive marketing team for three years.
Our instructor, Professor Kathie Amato had all kinds of incredible ideas to arouse people’s initiative. During the four-month’s study, in addition to the regular lectures, we watched countless fascinating commercials, had snacks to keep the colorful packages as name tags, did a double blind test among different brands soda which is exactly the thing I wished to do for a long time. And yes, my classmates continually surprised me with their creative point of views!
The cases we studied covered industries including food, transportation, medicine, travel and FMCG. We were required to deliver three slides after self studying each case. Then we would discuss, or should I say, fight against each other, about what we had digged out from the case. It was really insightful to have tens of people with all kinds of backgrounds sitting together, sharing opinions. I used to believe I was the one thinking out of the box. But here I realized that there is always a box out of my box!
At first it was not easy to squeeze all the conclusions into only three slides. Right, lack of concision is the sign of inexperience. But gradually, I could deal with not only the content itself, no matter it was quantitative analysis or qualitative statement, but also the artwork! I just have an obsession with well-arranged, good-looking slides design. Maybe that is one of the assets I have recieved from marketing experience.
