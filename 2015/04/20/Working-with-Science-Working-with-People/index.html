Working with Science, Working with People

By Lian.Liu

Apr 20 2015 Updated:May 4 2015

Contents

The presentation on Duke MEM Internship Assessment Showcase 2015 Spring.

Presentation Video

Today I would like to talk about working with science, and working with people. This is where my passion lies. Just like this program, engineering and management. It is easier to be expert in one field. But how to bring the greater energy from the combination, is a long learning process. I’m glad to share with you how I learned to work with science and work with people.


Back in college, I learned mainly from textbook. Then I joined work, where I learned from practice. Here at MEM, I believe you have the same feeling as I do, we learn from people.


This was me, four years ago, when I graduated from Fudan University. Looking back, this degree equipped me with basic qualities as an engineer; and today I am still benefiting from these skills. Here by textbook, it does not just mean paper book, or electronic versions. It means that life at campus is more straightforward comparing to real life.

I realized the fact when I volunteered at the world exposition in my junior year of college. We had held big events at campus before, but I still felt lost when facing so many people from all around the world. They are not like formulas that you can calculate or inference! why did they just get anxious or angry at me when I was performing my duty? That was the time I decided to learn more about people. Luckily, my experience at campus and internship help me land on a job in marketing. Where, I started to swift my learning focus from working with science to working with people.


This picture was two years ago when I was presenting our ecommerce project to my colleagues in Henkel. Henkel is a German chemical company famous for its industrial adhesive products. My job was in China marketing department, focusing on digital marketing and media relations. Here I’d like to share this online authentication project.


Fake product was a unique problem in China. We do not have much material to learn from other regions. In addition, this project involved various internal and external departments, which increased the potential conflicts. But communication and logistic issues can be easily overcame, even for a new professional. You just need to practice and learn fast. As for the lack of experience, we still have indirect experience to learn from. The distribution management department had dealt with offline authentication cases before; our legal department had close cooperation with legal-enforcement agencies; our sales team were familiar with the local market.

Another barriers in this cross department project was that people have different interests and appeals. Some department just want to shut down the fake website; some want to investigate deeper but they do not have enough budget to support. It is so important that we have marketing lead this project that we make sure all voices are heard and negotiate in between to convert conflict to contract, which eventually brought benefit to everyone.
The successful of this project created a marketing example in the company. Evidently it saved company losses and consolidated brand equity. Moreover, it enhanced the relationship between departments. Thanks to all the conflicts we had and the close cooperation afterwards.


I summarized the key takeaways from my three-year work experience in these four words.
Capability, is the one crucial but I do not worry about too much. We are engineers; we have solid technical background and we learn fast.
Adaptability, is another essential quality for new professionals. Things change quickly when dealing with people. And we keep meeting with new situations. My responsibilities from website and email marketing to mobile devices and ecommerce. I had never expected that my job could be anything about authentication.
Initiative works as complement of adaptability. A lot of successful business leaders I interviewed mentioned one thing in common that they keep close track to industrial news and actively participate in networking events. This enthusiasm, or curiosity guides you to prepare in advance.
Finally, no matter how cruel, stressful business world is, I’ still want to keep my genuineness. It wins me trust and respect. Genuineness also means that I am willing to accept who I am. Always have a clear idea of what I want. Just do not get lost among all kinds of values and temptations.


That is why I decided to come to this program, to know more about people like me. Here I learnt more than from textbook, more than practice. Because it is the engineers have taught me, how to work with people.

I love the classroom atmosphere. Not only in class, but also the discussions throughout our case study, projects and assignments. We exchange opinions openly and look at a problem from different perspectives.

Events outside the classroom it is much more than amusement.
I like it when people do not judge others here. During all the remarkable activities we’ve had here, people embrace the diversified opinions and encourage all kinds of success. This encouraging spirit in return creates more opportunities and a prosperous society.
I will graduate in one month, and I am really grateful to what I have learnt here. I took data mining and decision models; I also took management and practicum. With all these years’ experience, I believe I am more competitive in my dreaming career, working with science, working with people.
